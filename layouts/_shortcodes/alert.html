{{- /* alert just calls the callout shortcode so the two render identically — there is a single implementation, in callout.html. The only mapping is alert's "context" arg → callout's "type"; the body comes from either a self-closing text="…" attribute or the inner content (block form), and is handed to callout as its inner content. Calling the shortcode (rather than sharing a partial) means alert inherits callout's behavior wholesale, including the translation-snapshot output (alert reduces to a callout shortcode, matching the JA convention) and the list-safety handling. The legacy icon="…" override is dropped (no occurrences in content; callout derives the icon from type), as is role="alert". RenderString expands the callout shortcode in the constructed string. We always emit the block (open/body/close) form rather than a self-closing text="" attribute, so a body containing quotes or markdown can't break the attribute. */ -}} {{- $type := .Get "context" | default (.Get "type") -}} {{- $body := .Get "text" | default .Inner -}} {{- $call := cond (ne $type "") (printf "{{< callout type=%q >}}%s{{< /callout >}}" $type $body) (printf "{{< callout >}}%s{{< /callout >}}" $body) -}} {{- .Page.RenderString (dict "display" "block") $call -}}
